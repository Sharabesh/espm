IRA Self-Determination
  • 1953

    House Concurrent Resolution 108

    • This resolution called for the termination of federal trust responsibilities,essentially eliminating any view of tribal sovereignty by the US government
    • Surplus native land was divided up and placed into private hands. As a corollary, all indigenous peoples were made American citizens.
    • With the end of tribal sovereignty, many of indigenous origin relocated to large urban centers such as Denver, Los Angeles, San Francisco, Oakland, or Seattle.
  • 1953

    Public Law 280

    • Authorized state governments to enforce most regular criminal laws on reservations. This created substantial conflicts both with state governments who hd to patrol additional land without increased funding and for tribes who now faced greater sovereignty infringements regarding law enforcement.
  • 1956

    Indian Relocation Act of 1956

    • The Relocation Act of 1956 attempted to encourge indigenous peoples to leave tribal lands to spur greater assimilation into the general population.
    • The Relocation Act offered to pay moving expenses and provide elements of vocational training to those of inidigenous descent who sought to move into major cities.
    • This act further sought to forcefully integrate natives into the general population while removing their tribal affiliations
    • Overall 109 tribes were terminated and 2.5 million acres of trust land was removed from protected status. In addition, over 12,000 Natives lost their tribal affiliation.
