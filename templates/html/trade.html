A Period of Trade and Treatise 1789 - 1828

{# Home#} Removal and Reservation
  • 1787

    Commerce Clause of the US Constitution

    Federal government has the power to “regulate commerce with foreign nations, and among the several states, and with the Indian Tribes”

  • 1790

    Indian Intercourse Act

    • Established that no sale of indian land would be made without the approval of the united states
    • Established role of federal government as “necessary”

  • 1823

    Johnson V. MacIntosh

    • First step to coherent policy, no real native americans involved, two white men
    • Rights to convey title based on first rights to land (conveyance from tribes or conveyance from the crown) Doctrine of discovery (discoverer owns), government owns the land and can give, but the tribes still have occupancy rights, but no ownership
    • Implications of this case: occupancy rights of tribes, impaired sovereignty rights held by the tribes, guardianship theory -> government responsibility to protect tribal rights
