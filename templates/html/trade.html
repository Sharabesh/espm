A Period of Trade and Treatise 1789 - 1828

{# Home#} Removal and Reservation
  • 1787

    Commerce Clause of the US Constitution

    Federal government has the power to “regulate commerce with foreign nations, and among the several states, and with the Indian Tribes”

    This Legislation introduced Indian tribes as sovereign nations with which the federal government had the sole authority to arbitrate

  • 1790

    Indian Intercourse Act

    • Established that no sale of indian land would be made without the approval of the united states
    • Established the role of the federal government in all dealings with natives
  • 1823

    Johnson V. MacIntosh

    • In 1775, Thomas Johnson purchased lands directly from teh Piankeshaw Indian tribe under a proclamation by the King of England In 1818, however, M'Intosh purchased an overlapping plot of land from Congress. Johnson's heirs sued M'Intosh for the right to the land.
    • The Supreme Court determined that rights to convey title were based on the first rights to land (conveyance from tribes or conveyance from the crown) Critically under the Doctrine of Discovery the federal government owns the land and was allowed to redistribute it but the tribes only had occupancy rights rather than ownership rights.
    • This ruling substantially reduced tribal sovereignty rights by limiting the ownership rights of tribes to their own land. It also strengthened the guardianship theory of federal responsibility to the indigenous peoples
