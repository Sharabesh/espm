A Period of Removal and Reservation 1829 - 1886

Home Removal and Reservation
  • 1830

    Indian Removal Act of 1830

    • Allowed President Andrew Jackson to negotiate with tribes to push them west past the mississippi

  • 1831

    Cherokee Nation vs. Georgia

    • Further clarified federal relationship to tribes and trust relationship (domestic dependent nation -> wardship)
    • Claimed that tribes could not be recognized as a sovereign power, because they did not have the power to be one
    • Because the were not viewed as a foreign power, the federal government had to look out for them in some sense
    • This interpretation enhances an sense of tribal rights to land and some sort of sovereignty (division of sovereignty)

  • 1832

    Worcester Vs. Georgia

    • Court ruled that state law is inapplicable in Indian country absent an express grant of authority from Congress
    • Andrew Jackson refused to enforce decision

  • 1871

    Indian Appropriation Act

    • Native American tribes were no longer recognized as independent nations by the government
    • Marked the end of signing treaties with Native American Tribes
    • Government was more able to take lands from Native American tribes
