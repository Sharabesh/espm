Indian Self-Determination 1961 – 1985

Termination {# Removal and Reservation #}
  • 1975

    Indian Self-Determination Act

    • Indian tribes could contract to run Bureau of Indian Affairs of Indian Health Services programs for tribal members
    • “Self determination contract clause”
      • Tribe would step into the shoes of the federal government and use tribal employees to run federal programs with federal funding
      • Tribes have contracted around 1.6 billion in programs from the Indian Health Services
    • Rights were granted for those of indigenous origin to run elements of the gaming industry which has supplemented federal income substantially

  • 1978

    Santa Clara Pueblo vs Martinez

    • Tribal government enacted an ordinance that allowed children of Pueblo men to be enrolled even if the other parent was not Pueblo
    • Supreme Court refused to recognize federal authority to interfere in right of tribe to determine membership

  • 2009

    Tribal Law and Order Act

    • Recognized complex jurisdictional scheme that exists in the Indian Country has negative impact on the ability to provide public safety services to Indian communities and requires a high degree of coordination among tribal, federal, and state law enforcement.
    • This Obama-era legislation authorized more funding for tribal law-enforcement.
