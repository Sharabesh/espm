A Period of Allotment 1887 – 1932

Home Indian Removal Act
  • 1886

    General (Dawes) Allotment Act

    • A bill to support the assimilation of the natives
      • Focuses on acculturation → Adoption of protestant work ethic, etc
      • Carlyle Indian School
    • Calls for the division of tribal holdings into private allotments
    • Property rights: tribal community > private trust patent > private fee patent
    • Incorporation: acculturation > assimilation = path to citizenship
    • Trust doctrine: interpreted in terms of temporary wardship as a means of encouraging indian independence and eventually ending trust doctrine obligations
    • Outcomes
      • Land loss: taxes, private sale, auction
      • Dependency: land loss, acculturation
      • 65% loss of Indian land
      • Surplus land sales → Funds went to the Indian population

  • 1906

    Burke Act

    • Amended the General Allotment Act of 1906
