  • 1886

    General (Dawes) Allotment Act

    • A bill to support the assimilation of the natives
      • Focuses on acculturation with Adoption of protestant work ethic, English language, etc
      • Schools like the Carlyle Indian School were established in order to acculturate Indian children prior and strip away tribal affiliation
    • Called for the division of tribal holdings into private allotments
    • Acculturation was deemed for more important than assimilation on the path to citizenship
    • The Trust doctrine was interpreted in terms of temporary wardship as a means of encouraging indian independence and eventually ending trust doctrine obligations
    • Outcomes
      • Land loss: taxes, private sale, auction
      • Dependency: land loss, acculturation
      • 65% loss of Indian land
      • Increases in tribal wealth as wealth from land sales went directly to the tribes

  • 1886

    United States V Kagama

    • The supreme court upheld the constitutionality of the Major Crimes Act of 1885 which gave the federal courts jurisdiction in cases of Indian-Indian crimes. Thus the federal government constitutionally had jurisdiction over crimes executed on tribal lands.

  • 1903

    Lone Wolf v. Hitchcock

    • Lone Wolf claimed that Native American tribes under the Medicine Lodge Treaty had lands taken away, in violation of the treaty
    • U.S. courts declared that government had plenary power, which meant congress could unilaterally abrogate (get rid of) any previous claims

  • 1906

    Burke Act

    • Amended the General Allotment Act of 1906 to demand that the government must assess whether individuals were capable of acquiring patents to alloted land. This act also prevented many Indigenous peoples from directly gaining US citizenship
